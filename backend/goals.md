# Goal Tracking Systems

## Milestone System
For progression-based goals where you level up through defined stages.

- Define milestones (M1, M2, M3, etc.) with specific targets
- Each milestone has a progress bar (e.g., 5 successful attempts to fill)
- Performing at or above milestone level: +1 progress
- Performing below milestone level: -1 progress
- Once progress bar is full: 3 successful attempts unlocks the next milestone
- At 0 progress, 3 consecutive failures: demote to previous milestone

## Abstinence System
For goals requiring complete avoidance of a behavior.

- Each day clean: +1 progress
- Slip (any use): resets streak to 0, but total clean days are preserved separately
- Milestones based on consecutive days (7, 30, 60, 90, 180 days)
- Optional: track cravings resisted and triggers identified

## XP System
For ongoing habit-based goals requiring consistent practice.

- Define activities with XP values
- First activity of the day: full XP
- Second+ activity same day: half XP (rounded down)
- After 3 days of no activity: meter decreases by 1 per day
- Milestones at XP thresholds (e.g., 10, 25, 50, 100)

## Quest System
For outcome-based goals requiring consistent effort toward concrete achievements.

Effort (XP):
- Define activities with XP values
- First activity of the day: full XP
- Second+ activity same day: half XP (rounded down)
- After 3 days of no activity: meter decreases by 1 per day

Milestones (define per goal):
- Stages (linear): Stage 1 → 2 → 3 → Complete (e.g., job search)
- Counts (cumulative): 1 → 3 → 5 → 10 achievements (e.g., PRs merged)

## Goal: I want to improve my mental well-being with ACT (XP System)

Accomplishment: I will develop consistent ACT practices to improve psychological flexibility and mental well-being.
Actions:        Complete ACT books/courses, practice daily mindfulness/defusion exercises, identify personal values and commit to value-aligned actions, and journal about progress.

M (Measurable)  - What data will measure the goal?How much? How well?

ACT-meter with XP system:
- Activities: reading/listening (+2 XP), exercises (+3 XP), journaling (+2 XP)
- First activity of the day: full XP
- Second+ activity same day: half XP (rounded down)
- After 3 days of no activity: meter decreases by 1 per day
- Milestones: 10 (Started), 25 (Building), 50 (Consistent), 100 (Integrated)

A (Achievable)  - Is the goal doable? Do you have the necessary skills and resources?

The goal is achievable with consistent practice. Resources: ACT books (e.g., "The Happiness Trap" by Russ Harris), online courses, apps, or a therapist if needed. 15-20 minutes daily is sufficient.

R (Relevant)    - How does the goal align with broader goals? Why is the result important?

ACT directly supports all other goals - it builds willpower, helps manage cravings (smoking/alcohol), reduces avoidance, and improves commitment to difficult tasks (marathon training, job search, college). Mental clarity enhances every area of life.

T (Time-Bound)  - What is the time frame for acomplishing this goal?

Reach "Consistent" level (50) within 2 months. Maintain above "Building" (25) for 3 consecutive months.
